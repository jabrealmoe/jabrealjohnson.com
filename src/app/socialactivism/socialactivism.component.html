Getting Involved in Community Activities as a Citizen

Maybe we can't all be as influential as Martin Luther King, Ghandi or Barack Obama; nevertheless, as citizens of a country and community, we have a responsibility to make our communities better! Change begins at home is a well known proverb, but too often forgotten because of existing commitments and poor priortization of the impact of community contributions. Sometimes involvement can be a as an activist in a protest to encourage change, more often than not those efforts can be leveraged in community centers and around higher learning institutions teaching STEM subjects to those who have a desire or need to change.



The Rebel

I had a place in Edina, Minnesota during the George Floyd incident, some 3.6 miles away to the mark. As a person of color having traveled throughout the USA and beyond, I've experenced prejudice by the police on a few occasions, but something about seeing this event on camera woke up America, and brought about a consciousness in many regarding the need to reform, and say "No!".

The Reformer

As citizens its equally important to use mainstream instituions to cut through redtape and ultimately achieve a movements objectives. To that end, we must continue to to lobby, promote referenda in support of a movements agenda, support peaceful protests, rallies and any other means to support reforms and enjoy our communities.

The Change Agent

Organizing people in communities around common goals that create power, and those are derived from education, which leads to power to affect policies. Furthermore, we want to promote education that involves majority of citizens and includes the whole society on the issue(s). This leads to long term structures in place that have an organic--grasss roots origin with long term benefits.
